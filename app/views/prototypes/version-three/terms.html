DfE Data Protection Policy

You must agree to:

  • ensure that only personal data which is necessary for a specific purpose is processed.
  • ensure that the minimum amount of personal data is collected and processed for a specific purpose.
  • ensure the processing is limited to that necessary for each purpose.
  • ensure the data is stored no longer than necessary.
  • complete a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)1 where processing presents a high risk to the rights and freedoms of data subjects.
  • complete staff training on how to comply with Data Protection Law and keeping a record of training completed.
  • report any losses or suspected breaches of personal data to the Information Commissioner within 72 hours. When the personal data breach is likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, we are required by law to notify the affected individuals without delay.
